To accommodate Ellsberg-type violations of additive probability measures, Schmeidler (1984, 1989) and Gilboa (1987) adopted  Choquet (1953-1954) integration as a representational form. We refer to this as Choquet expected utility. Schmeidler's  axiomatization is based on lottery acts (functions from the states of the world into probability distributions on the  consequence space) introduced by Anscombe and Aumann (1963). Gilboa used Savage's basic formulation (see Savage, 1954, and  Fishburn, 1970), so that the set of states is continuously divisible. Recently, Wakker (1989a, b) developed topological  approaches when the set of states is finite, and Nakamura (1990a) and Wakker (1991) developed algebraic approaches for that  case.  Quiggin (1982) proposed anticipated utility to accommodate Allais-type violations of von Neumann-Morgenstern expected utility.  His representational form is based on expectations of gambles with respect to their distorted (de)cumulative distributions.  Because of this, Quiggin's utility is sometimes called rank-dependent utility. Several axiomatic and descriptive developments  of anticipated utility are available, including Chew (1989), Chew, Karni, and Safra (1987), Chew and Epstein (1989b), Hilton  (1988), Quiggin (1985), Segal (1989), and Yaari (1987). Those theories presume that the underlying consequence space is the  real line. Recently, Nakamura (1990b) provided an axiomatic characterization for arbitrary consequence spaces.  Wakker (1990a) discussed the equivalence of Choquet expected utility and anticipated utility. The present paper discusses an  application of multisymmetric structures to argue that a common algebraic feature provides an axiomatic characterization for  both Choquet expected utility and anticipated utility. CONCLUSION  The main purpose of this paper has been to introduce the weak and strong forms of multisymmetric structures and then to  develop their numerical representations. The multisymmetric structures postulate an n-ary operation to generalize a binary  operation in bisymmetric structures. We also developed numerical representations for multiple operations and then applied them  to derive a common axiomatic characterization for Choquet expected utility and Quiggin's anticipated utility.
