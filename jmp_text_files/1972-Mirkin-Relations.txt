The representation theorem for the relation "more than" arising for nonexact measurements with errors which may vary for  different objects is demonstrated. Some new representation theorems for associated relations are given. The set-theoretic  structure of the relations in question is revealed.  1. Thus, we may define semiorders as irreflexive relations satisfying conditions (3) and (4).  These conditions have clear interpretations in terms of the ends of real-line intervals. Condition (3) says that the right end  of one of each of two intervals is equal to or lies to the right of the right end of the other interval. Condition (4) says  that, for each two intervals a and b, either the right end of a lies to the right of (or equals) the right end of b, or the  left end of the interval a is to the left of (or equals) the left end of b.
