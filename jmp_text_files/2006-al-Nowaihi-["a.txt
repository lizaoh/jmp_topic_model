It is now generally accepted that decision makers overweight low probabilities and underweight high probabilities. Thus they behave as if they transform the objective (cumulative) probability distribution using an inverted S-shaped function ( Quiggin, 1982, 1993; Tversky & Kahneman, 1992 ). A number of weighting functions have been proposed. However, the first axiomatically derived weighting function was that of  Prelec (1998) . His main axiom was  compound invariance .  Luce (2001)  proposed the simpler and more easily testable assumption of  reduction invariance . He also provided a simpler derivation of Prelec's function.
