JOURNAL OF MATHEMATICAL PSYCHOLOGY 27, 325 (1983)  Note  A Comment on the Development of the Theory of "Direct" Psychophysics  DAVID H. KRANTZ  Bell Laboratories  In a theoretical paper on magnitude estimation (Krantz, 1972), 1 noted that the basic idea of "relation theory," namely, that  subjective scaling is based on a property of stimulus pairs, was not new. I cited published work by Helson and by Stevens and  unpublished papers by Kristof and Shepard, giving special credit to the latter. Since Shepard's paper was the main source of  my ideas, and since it also makes a valuable contribution, I was happy to witness its publication in revised form (1978) and  reprinting (1981).  The reprinting of Shepard's paper has led, however, to some correspondence concerning historical priority for the idea. In  particular, neither Shepard nor I cited the contribution by Junge (1966, 1967). My crediting of Shepard as the source for my  own work was not intended to express an opinion about historical priority. The idea in question emerged gradually. A form of  it is found in Hering's work; it was applied specifically to magnitude estimation by Stevens (1957), though with little  emphasis and no development; a somewhat more explicit and partly developed version was published by Junge; and Shepard's  development was the most thorough and most clearly testable. My own contribution was clarification, of the sort that sometimes  results from foundational analysis.  REFERENCES  JUNGE, K. Some problems of measurement in psychophvsics.
