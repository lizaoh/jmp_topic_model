6. CONCLUDING REMARKS 

Naive observation suggests that people’s beliefs are plagued by minor inconsistencies, but infrequently suffer from major inconsistencies. The axioms presented here model appoximate consistency without imposing the full computational burden of exact representability. The small size of probability “errors” reinforces the intuition that there may be little motivation to refine beliefs to eliminate all inconsistencies. These ideas have been developed above and probably could be recaptured along with additional desirable properties. 

The second example shows that even though a qualitative probability structure fails to be representable over a coarse algebra it may have a close approximation when finer algebras are considered. The basic insight is to split the atoms of the Kraft ef al. example into smaller atoms that permit a close approximation.
