A metric transform will be called respectively monotone,  continuous, or nontrivial when the function 0 is strictly increasing, continuous, or not identically zero. A monotone metric  transform preserves all pair comparison-relations. Thus a proponent of Euclidean metrics 11 x - y 11, might hope a suitable  metric transform of any admissible system (X p) would imbed the same comparison data in some Euclidean space. The uniqueness  of pseudoconvex metrics (Lew, 1975) does not contradict the possibility of such transforms, because a subset of a familiar  space need not be a pseudoconvex space under the induced metric.
