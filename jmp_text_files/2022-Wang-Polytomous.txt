Knowledge space theory (KST) was introduced by  Doignon and Falmagne (1985) . It is a set-theoretical framework for assessing individual knowledge states and providing guidance for further learning ( Falmagne & Doignon, 2011 ). A knowledge state represents a set of items that an individual has mastered, and the responses to items are only categorized as correct or incorrect. KST needs to be generalized to classify the responses into more than two alternatives.  Schrepp (1997)  first generalized the central KST concepts to items with multiple answer alternatives, and replaced the binary case used in ordinary KST with a linearly ordered scale.  Bartl and Belohlavek (2011)  accounted for graded knowledge states from the perspective of a complete residuated lattice in fuzzy logic.  Stefanutti et al. (2020)  mainly followed the work of  Schrepp (1997)  and extended the approach to response categories forming a complete lattice. Recently,  Heller (2021)  has integrated the approaches of  Schrepp (1997)  and  Stefanutti et al. (2020)  into his polytomous framework and provided a generalized theory of polytomous quasi-ordinal knowledge spaces. Their contributions are significant to the polytomous extensions of knowledge space theory.

This paper focuses on the recent work done by  Heller (2021)  on the one hand and by  Stefanutti et al. (2020)  on the other hand.  Heller (2021)  provided precedence relations that form particular quasi-orders on an extended set of (virtual) items. Via setting up a Galois connection, precedence relations were put in a one-to-one correspondence with certain collections  
                         K 
                       of polytomous knowledge states. All those  
                         K 
                       are complete lattices, meaning that suprema (joins) and infima (meets) of elements from  
                         K 
                       also exist in  
                         K 
                      . In particular, the component-directed joins proposed by  Heller (2021)  can be obtained item-wise. However, this conclusion may not hold for a general polytomous knowledge structure. Our first counterexample shows that the assumptions in  Heller (2021)  do not guarantee component-directed joins to be defined item-wise. This leads to an incomplete characterization of the closed elements of the Galois connection in Proposition 8 of  Heller (2021) . This paper provides theoretical results resolving that issue in Section  2 .

An essential contribution of  Stefanutti et al. (2020)  is that they provided a sufficient and necessary characterization of the strict closure through their atoms and basis.  Stefanutti et al. (2020)  stated that the basis of a polytomous structure is the collection of all its atoms, and the atoms are equivalent to  
                         ⨆ 
                      -irreducible elements from the polytomous structure. However, a counterexample shows that a  
                         ⨆

                         V
