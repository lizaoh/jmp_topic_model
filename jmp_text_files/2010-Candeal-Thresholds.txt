This kind of numerical representation of semiordered structures is encountered in a wide range of applications, as extensive measurement in Mathematical Psychology (see  Krantz, 1967; Lehrer, & Wagner, 1985 ), choice theory under risk (see  Fishburn, 1968 ), decision-making under risk (see  Rubinstein, 1988 ), or modellization of choice with errors (see  Agaev & Aleskerov, 1993 ).

It was proved in  Scott and Suppes (1958)  (see also  Rabinovitch, 1977 ) that every semiordering defined on a finite set  
                         S 
                       actually admits a Scott–Suppes representation.

The case where the set  
                         S 
                       is infinite has also been analyzed by several authors. For the particular case when  
                         S 
                       is countable, a characterization of the Scott–Suppes representability was obtained in  Manders (1981)  as well as in  Beja and Gilboa (1992) . (See  Lemma 3.5  below.)

Several authors have already commented upon the difficulties that the analysis of the uncountable case carries (see, e.g., Section 4 in  Beja and Gilboa (1992) ), sometimes avoiding the consideration of a Scott–Suppes representation (that could be very restrictive, as stated on p. 126 in  S̀wistak (1980) ), and substituting it by milder forms of representations (e.g., through different kinds of interval representations) that are more easily characterized. Nevertheless, the existence of a Scott–Suppes representation immediately implies the existence of many other kinds of representations analyzed in the literature (e.g., interval representations in the sense of  Fishburn (1973) , GNR representations in the sense of  Beja and Gilboa (1992) ). However, as far as we know, no complete characterization of the Scott–Suppes representability of semiorders has been given, for the general case, in the papers mentioned above.

More recently, several complete characterizations of the existence of Scott–Suppes representations of semiorders, valid for the general case, were finally achieved in  Candeal, Induráin, and Zudaire (2002) . But an important difficulty still remains, namely, all the characterizations presented in  Candeal et al. (2002)  lean on the consideration of some extension of a totally preordered structure that is closely related to the given semiorder. Actually, some of the relevant conditions that appear in those characterizations of the Scott–Suppes representability of semiorders are phrased in terms of properties of the suitable related extensions. The main trouble is that the extensions could be difficult to visualize, and are not determined “a priori” in an easy standard manner. Therefore, it seems necessary to look for alternative conditions for the Scott–Suppes representability of semiorders that are internal. That is, they should be expressed in terms of the given semiorder (only). Partial results in this direction have been recently obtained in  Abrísqueta, Candeal, Induráin, and Zudaire (2009) .

The goal of the present paper is to furnish an internal characterization, for the general case, of the Scott–Suppes representability of semiorders.
