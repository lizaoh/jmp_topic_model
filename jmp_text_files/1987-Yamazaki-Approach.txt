The apparent phenomena are mostly determined only by a relationship between a position of light  stimulus and that of an observer, which may be called frameless (Indow, 1974). According to these results, apparently regular  paterns are found to have been constructed, in physical space as 3-dimensional Euclidean space, with a kind of distortion  whose characteristics define a visual space. In particular, on the basis of Blumenfeld's finding (1913), Luneburg described  the geometrical properties of the binocular visual space by a metric Riemannian space of negative constant curvature.  Since then experimental support for the theory has been obtained (Hardy, Rand, & Rittler, 1951; Zajaczkowska, 1956; Hagino and  Yoshioka,- 1976; Indow and Watanabe, 1984, etc.), and some proposals for the improvement of the theory have been made, e.g.,  on the sign of Gaussian curvature (Squires, 1956; Blank, 1961), other metric functions (Blank, 1953; Foley, 1966), the mapping  function (Nishikawa, 1969; Indow, 1982), local transformations between the physical and visual configurations (Shipley, 1957;  Blank, 1958; Indow, 1974), a new method to evaluate the discrepancy between the two alleys (Indow, 1979). These are mostly  concerned with the metric properties of the visual space and are still within Luneburg's theory.  The modelling of the binocular visual space by Luneburg was a great achievement in its time. However, the negative constant  curvature is not necessarily consistent with experimental results (Hardy et al., 1951; Ishii, 1962; Hagino & Yoshioka, 1976).  In Luneburg's model, a perceptual straight line is given by a geodesic in Riemannian space. The psychological meaning of  equidistance corresponds to the fact that the lengths of the geodesics between each pair of light points are equal, but the  psychological meaning of parallelism is not obvious in the space. In Luneburg's theory, a visual space is obtained by  transforming a physical space with bipolar coordinate into a cyclopean egocentered space with orthogonal coordinate (~, q) by  the mapping function (Fig. 1). Then, parallel alley and horopter were formulated by  paths with the asymmetric connection given by Eq. (20).  Computer simulations were carried out to test the model. On the other hand, another experimental  interpretation of Theorem B may be applied to equipartitioned parallel alley (Hardy et. al., 1953), as indicated by Indow  (1985).  To facilitate the geometrical treatment of the proposed model, it was assumed in Option 2 that visual image deformation is  small as given by Eq. (29).
