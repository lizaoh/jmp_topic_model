Finally, we remark on a generalization of our theorem motivated by some remarks of Krantz made in a review of visual scaling  (Krantz, 1972). Krantz considers the general Thurstone model,  P"v = F ( ['(x) - F'(y) E(x, y) )  (15)  with E2(x, y) = u2(x) -r- a2(y) - 2a(x)a(y)p(x, y). In other words, the assumption of stochastic independence is relaxed; p is  the correlation of the variables X(x), X(y) appearing in the random representation (2). Krantz notes that there are solutions  of (15)  compatible with (1) if for some suitable function h, p = h o P. The solutions he produces are characterized by the linear  constraint (5) between It and a. It can be shown by considerations entirely similar to those used in the proof of our theorem  that this linear constraint is both necessary and sufficient for (nontrivial) solutions of (1), (15), to exist. This result is  remarkable in that even though there may be statistical dependence between the random variables X(x), X(y) it is possible,  under certain circumstances, to transform it completely away. At the time of this writing I know, however, no natural  interpretation for Krantz' condition-that the correlation p(x, y) be a function of the binary choice probabilities P.,,,, .
