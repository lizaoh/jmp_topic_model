An influential theorem, Green’s area theorem  (Green, 1964; Green & Swets, 1966) , also known as the 2AFC area theorem (  Macmillan & Creelman, 2005 , pp. 170–171), plays an important role in signal detection theory (SDT). It reveals the inherent relationship between the yes–no (YN) and the two-alternative forced-choice (2AFC) paradigms. The area ( AUC ) under the receiver operating characteristic (ROC) curve with the  hit  probability ( H ) and the  false-alarm  probability ( F ) in the YN equals the proportion of correct responses (

It establishes a link between the two psychophysical paradigms: YN and 2AFC, and a link among the sensory measures:  AUC ,

An assumption in Green’s area theorem and the extended Green’s area theorem in this theoretical note is that there is no response bias in the forced-choice methods including the 2AFC and the general specified ‘ 
                         M 
                         + 
                         N

Response bias is an important factor in decision-making process. It is noted that  DeCarlo (2012)  developed a general model for  m  AFC with bias. However, the models for general forced-choice methods with response bias have not been developed due to the complexity of the models. It is not clear if Green’s area theorem can also be extended to the forced-choice methods with response bias. Further research is needed.
