A formalization is given that involves explicit  consideration of the outcome space in the definition of a learning model, and it is shown that it is possible to obtain from  the learning process a scale over uncertain outcomes that is formally Similar to a von Neumann-Morgenstern utility scale.  INTRODUCTION  Luce (1964) gave a definition of a (real) one-parameter family of learning operators. Such a family is, in fact, a semigroup  of operators on a real interval with a second real interval as parameter space. In this paper an abstract one-parameter family  of learning operators.is defined in such a way that it has essentially the same properties as a (real) one-parameter family;  the major exception is that in the present definition the real intervals of Luce's definition are replaced by abstract sets.  It is intended that the parameter space of an abstract family be identified with the set of possible outcomes, one of which is  delivered to the subject on each trial of a learning experiment.  The body of the paper concerns sufficient conditions for an abstract family to be quasi-additive, this property being closely  related to quasi-multiplicativity as defined by Luce (1964). The final section of the paper shows that a theorem proved by  Lamperti and Suppes (1960) for the beta model is in fact true for a broad class of quasi-additive families. Applying this  theorem to a two-choice situation with contingent reinforcement we show that it is possible to obtain from the learning  process a scale over uncertain outcomes that is formally similar to a von NeumannMorgenstern utility scale.  1 This wark was supported in part by National Science Foundation grant GB1462 to the University of Pennsylvania and was  completed while the author was a Fellow of the Miller Institute for Basic Research in Science at the University of California,  Berkeley.  * Present address: The University of Alberta, Edmonton, Canada.  414  ABSTRACT FAMILIES OF LEARNING OPERATORS 415  ONE-PARAMETER FAMILIES  In the following, when X is a set, < a weak ordering relation on X, and x, y G X, then x == y if and only if x < y and y < x,  and x - v if and only if x is identical to Y.
