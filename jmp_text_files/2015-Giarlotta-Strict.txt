Another alternative approach that is widely explored in the literature employs either (i) a complete weak preference

In this setting, the associated indifference is in general intransitive, a feature that is natural in many decision problems ( Fishburn, 1970a,b; Shafer, 1974; Tversky, 1969 ). The most studied binary relations of this type are (weak or strict)  interval orders  ( Fishburn, 1970b, 1985 ) and (weak or strict)  semiorders  ( Luce, 1956; Pirlot & Vincke, 1997 ). The weak and the strict preference approaches are fully equivalent for these two types of preference structures, since one can go back and forth from their weak version to their strict version by using the process of canonical reduction/completion.

NaP-preferences are strictly related to classical preference relations. In fact, total preorders, interval orders, and semiorders are the possible components of special types of NaP-preferences ( Giarlotta, 2014; Giarlotta & Watson, 2014 ). Furthermore, using suitable extensions of the Ferrers and the semitransitivity properties, one can define enhanced versions of interval orders and semiorders, called  strong interval orders  and  strong semiorders .

Section   4  summarizes the findings of this paper, and suggests future directions of research.

In this paper we have studied the relationship between notions of NaP-preferences and the existence of suitable families of order relations. The rationale for our approach is given by  Lemma 4 , which says that any nonempty set of total preorders gives rise to a NaP-preference, and vice versa. Therefore, it becomes interesting to study collectionwise properties of families of binary relations that are suitably connected to NaP-preferences. The notions of an injective and projective family of binary relations are designed exactly in this way, as emphasized by  Theorem 3 .

Future research on the topic points in three main directions. First of all, as a natural continuation of this analysis, we wish to study collectionwise properties of special families of total preorders, which are associated to NaP-preferences satisfying enhanced properties of mixed transitivity (e.g.,  internal  and  external  transitivity: see  Giarlotta, 2014 ).

Second, both  Lemma 4  and  Theorem 3  point to a notion of dimension for NaP-preferences and normalized NaP-preferences (defined as the smallest cardinality of a set of binary relations as in the statement of those results). It could be interesting to relate the resulting notions of dimension to the classical notions of dimension and width of order relations.
