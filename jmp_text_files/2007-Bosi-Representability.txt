Interval orders were introduced by  Fishburn (1970a, 1970b)  as a tool to model intransitive indifference relations, following the path initiated in the seminal paper of  Luce (1956)  in which the idea of semiorders (that are a particular case of interval orders) had already appeared together with its motivation in the modelling of preferences. As a matter of fact, the concept of an interval order was already implicit in some papers dated in the 1910s (see, e.g.  Wiener, 1914; Fishburn & Monjardet, 1992 ). The problem of the existence of a numerical representation of such structures by means of two real-valued functions was thoroughly studied in  Fishburn (1970a, 1970b, 1973, 1985)  and  Doignon et al. (1984) . The continuity of such a representation was studied by  Chateauneuf (1987)  when the set of object is supposed to be a connected topological space. The aim of the paper is to give conditions ensuring that an interval order defined on a set endowed with a topological structure has a continuous numerical representation by means of two real-valued functions. Our results make central use of the possibility to associate a weak order to an interval order as shown in  Doignon et al. (1984)  in their study of biorders.
