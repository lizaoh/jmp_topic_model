ARNOLD  Iowa State University, Ames, Iowa 50010  Stimulus sampling theory is extended to cover the case of a continuum of responses in which the response is given continuously  in time. The case of noncontingent Poissontype reinforcement is studied in detail, and some attention is given to contingent  schedules. Two axiom systems suggested by Suppes and Donio are examined together with a third possible axiomitization.  Throughout the paper, nonuniform sampling probabilities for the stimuli are considered.  1. INTRODUCTION  Suppes and Donio (1967) studied the N-element pattern model in the case of continuous time and two-response choices. They  obtained the response distribution, assuming noncontingent Poisson reinforcement. In addition, they developed the theory for a  one-element model with a continuum of responses. Donio (1964) discussed the theory for the two-response situation assuming  simple contingent Poisson reinforcement. The reader is referred to Suppes and Donio (1967) for a discussion of the motivation  behind these models.  The present note is concerned with extensions of their results. Section 2 will cover the N-element model with a continuum of  responses under noncontingent Poisson reinforcement. In Section 3 we present the results for the two-response experiment with  unequal sampling frequencies and non-contingent reinforcement. These are easily derived using the methods of Section 2, and  represent a minor increase of generality over the earlier results of Suppes and Donio (1967). Section 4 is devoted to a  discussion of contingent reinforcement schedules.  Suppes and Donio (1967) suggested two possible axiom systems for the continuoustime pattern model. We will derive the response  distribution for both axiom systems, as well as for a third modification of the axiom system.  2.
