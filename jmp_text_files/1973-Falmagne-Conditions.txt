FALMAGNE  Department of Psychology, New York University  This paper discusses a sufficient set of conditions for a structure to be ordered-ring isomorphic to a positive subinterval of  the real numbers. It extends our previous results concerning bounded versions of Holder's Theorem (Falmagne, 1971). The main  result can serve as a basic lemma for establishing representation theorems in polynomial conjoint measurement when the  empirical sets contain neither large elements nor ones close to the natural zero.  The proof of representation theorems, in measurement theory, is often ultimately based on a small number of basic results  regarding the (order, group or ring) structure of the real number system. In polynomial conjoint measurement (Krantz, Luce,  Suppes and Tversky, 1971), the relevant lemma is the following classical result: any Archimedean fully ordered-ring is  isomorphic to a unique subring of the real number field (Fuchs, 1963). If realistic measurement axioms are required, this  result, however, will not always be adequate. An example of difficulty is if one or more of the empirical sets of the  polynomial conjoint measurement structure contains neither large elements nor ones close to the natural zero. What is required  in such a case is a system of axioms characterizing whatever remains of the Archimedean ordered-ring structure, when its  isomorphism is restricted to some bounded subset of the real numbers. Exactly what kind of bounded subset will be made clear  in the definition below. The purpose of this note is to provide such a characterization.  A companion paper is Falmagne (1971). In fact, the theorem given here leads immediately to a natural extension to rings of  Theorem 2 in that paper which concerns groups. This result can also be regarded as an extension of Krantz et al., Theorem 2.6  (1971, p. 55), itself an extension of Fuchs' Theorem (1963, p. 126) cited above. To discuss our problem more precisely, we introduce some  terminology.  DEFINITION. A (real) subinterval is an order-connected subset of the ordered additive group of the real numbers. In other  terms, a set C is a subinterval iff there exists a subgroup G of the additive reals which contains C such that whenever z E G,  x < z < y and x, y E C, then z E C. A subinterval C is positive iff x > 0 for all x E C. It is nondegenerate iff there exists  x E C such that 3x E C.  Problem I . Find conditions for the existence of an ordered-ring isomorphism onto a positive, nondegenerate subinterval.  In a previous paper (Falmagne, 1971), 1 gave a set of necessary and sufficient conditions for a structure to be ordered-group  isomorphic to a positive, nondegenerate subinterval. Such an isomorphism is unique up to a multiplication by a positive  constant. Problem I is thus partly solved.
