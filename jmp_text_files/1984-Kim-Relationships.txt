In order for it to be a  multiplicative homomorphism it will suffice that the product of any two nonzero blocks by nonzero.  Pattison (1982) asked what conditions on the block guarantee this. She found several: (1) the rows (or columns) of each  nonzero block contain at least one 1; (2) there is a main diagonal entry in each main diagonal block such that the row (and  column) through that entry is the sum of all rows through that block; (3) what she calls sender and receiver set conditions.  The first condition is invariant under rearrangements of rows and columns. The receiver set condition is invariant under  simultaneous rearrangements of rows and columns which are the identity on the set of columns that are zero for the whole  semigroup.
