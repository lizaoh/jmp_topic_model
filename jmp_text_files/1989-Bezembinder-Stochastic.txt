In a Monte Carlo study of Zinnes and Griggs' (1974) multidimensional probabilistic unfolding model for preferential choice  (hereafter abbreviated as ZG-model) DeSoete, Carroll, and DeSarbo (1986) generated 20,000 sets of the three pairwise choice  probabilities among three stimuli. Since they failed to observe a single violation of strong stochastic transitivity (SST)  they concluded: "... it seems that [the ZG-model] implies strong stochastic transitivity ..." (DeSoete et al., 1986, p. 37).  We will show analytically that, indeed, the ZG-model enjoys SST.
