RousH Mathematics Research Group, Alabama State University  We show that a condition of Gardenfors regarding preferences on subsets of a set is impossible to satisfy for weak orders in  general but is possible to satisfy for total orders.  Suppose we know an individual's preference ordering on the elements of a set U. This preference ordering is assumed to be  complete and transitive, that is, a weak order. In many methods of social choice, the social choice can be a subset of U  having more than one element. Presumably the final choice is made at random from this subset, although (here) the individual  does not know the probabilities that different members of the subset will be chosen. When will he prefer one finite subset of  U to another finite subset of U? A number of sufficient conditions have been proposed. For instance, Packard (1979) discusses  five methods.  In particular Packard observed that none of his five methods had a certain property introduced by Gardenfors (1976).
