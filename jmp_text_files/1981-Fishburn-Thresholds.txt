INTRODUCTION  This paper considers numerical threshold models for finite ordered sets that lie between the constant-threshold model for  semiorders and the variable-threshold model for interval orders. Each intermediate model allows a fixed number n > 2 of  threshold levels or interval lengths. Our aim is to prove that, unlike the case for semiorders and interval orders, the  classes of finite interval orders that are homomorphically representable by the intermediate models are not axiomatizable by a  universal sentence in first-order logic. Other classes of finite relational systems that are known to be nonaxiomatizable by a  universal sentence include ordered difference systems (Scott & Suppes, 1958), relational structures for additive conjoint  measurement and subjective expected utility (Titiev, 1972), and partial orders of dimension n for n > 2 (Baker, Fishburn, &  Roberts, 1970).
