Conjoint structures play a significant role in the theory of measurement. In the most often treated 2-component case, a conjoint structure is a relational structure 〈 X 
                      1 , X 
                      2 ,≼〉, where ≼ is a weak order on the Cartesian product  X

While this notion was first introduced by  Narens (1981) , the term factorizable automorphisms was coined by  Luce and Cohen (1983) , and most of the results on the subject are due to them. The importance of the factorizable automorphisms is somewhat obscured by the fact that a 2-component conjoint structure is not properly formalized by the relational structure 〈 X 
                      1 , X 
                      2 ,≼〉. Once this is remedied, the remaining automorphisms are factorizable (cf.  Luce, Krantz, Suppes, & Tversky, 1990 ). The point is that not only the weak order ≼ is to be preserved, but also the identity of the component sets  X

Suck (1990, 1998)  provided sufficient conditions for the existence of a conjoint representation of an  〈A, O 〉 -structure. His constructive proof proceeds by establishing a partial order  ⊑  on  
                         O 
                      , which captures the hierarchy of the ‘marginal’ orderings, and gives rise to a structure  〈 O ,⊑〉 . Each weak order in

From this point of view it seems natural to investigate the automorphisms of both structures  〈A, O 〉  and  〈 O ,⊑〉 . It has already been shown that the automorphisms of  〈A, O 〉  correspond to the factorizable automorphisms of the derived conjoint structure ( Suck, 1990 ). In order to characterize the automorphisms of  〈 O ,⊑〉 , which reflect the non-uniqueness inherent in the construction of the product structure,  Suck (1998)  introduced the wider class of the so-called generalized factorizable automorphisms.

The present paper provides a grouptheoretic characterization of the class of generalized factorizable automorphisms in  n -component conjoint structures. Previous results obtained for the 2-component case are generalized. This sets the frame for characterizing solvability by properties of automorphisms, for deriving results on the existence of factorizable automorphisms, and for relating the generalized factorizable automorphisms to the isomorphisms of concatenation structures induced on the components of a conjoint structure.

To summarize we have presented a characterization of the group of generalized factorizable automorphisms in conjoint structures.  Suck (1998)  introduced this class of automorphisms extending the group of factorizable automorphisms, and presented some preliminary results for the 2-component case. These results are generalized in various respects. First, we treat the case of  n -component conjoint structures ( n -CCS) for any finite number  n . The results even hold for conjoint structures with an infinite, but countable number of components. Identifying  I  with the set of positive integers, we simply have to replace the symmetric group  
                         S 
                         n 
                       at each of its occurrences by the group  
                         S 
                         ∞ 
                       of permutations of  I  with finite support (the support of a permutation is the set of elements not fixed by it). Second, the role and nature of the involved solvability assumptions is clarified. This leads to the distinction between weak and strict solvability. Third, the class of generalized factorizable automorphisms  
                         G 
                       of an additive  n -CCS is proved to be the semi-direct product  
                         G = F ⊙ E 
                       of its normal subgroup of factorizable automorphisms  
                         F 
                      , and a subgroup  
                         E 
                       isomorphic to the symmetric group  
                         S 
                         n 
                       of degree  n . This sets the frame for discussing how solvability assumptions are mirrored by properties of automorphisms, for deriving results on the existence of factorizable automorphisms, and for relating the generalized factorizable automorphisms of a  n -CCS to the isomorphisms of the concatenation structures induced on its components.

As the results indicate, the group of generalized factorizable automorphisms  
                         G 
                       does not provide any contribution to the independence of factors of a  n -CCS other than that captured by the group of factorizable automorphisms  
                         F 
                      . It merely adds information related to solvability, which is represented by the group  
                         E 
                      . Within  Suck's (1990, 1998)  approach of deriving a conjoint representation for a family of weak orders on a given set, the group  
                         E 
                       reflects the lack of uniqueness of the ordering of the component sets in the constructed Cartesian product. In any case, the established isomorphism of the groups  
                         E 
                       and  
                         S 
                         n 
                       implies that the general  n -component case is inherently different from the 2-component case, because the symmetric group of degree  n  is Abelian for  n =2, but not for  n ⩾3.
