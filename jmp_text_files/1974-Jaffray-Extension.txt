A stronger necessary condition, H, interpreted as both an independence and Archimedean condition, is  derived. It is shown to be sufficient when X is countable by constructing an additive utility as the limit of a sequence of  additive utilities on finite subsets of X. When X is not countable, but is a Cartesian product, another necessary condition,  the existence of A, a countable perfectly (order-) dense subset of X, is added to H; an additive utility is constructed by  extension to X of an additive utility on a countable set linked to A. INTRODUCTION  The problem of the existence of an additive utility on a weakly ordered Cartesian product (or subset of it), also known as the  problem of additive conjoint measurement, has already received many contributions. Necessary and sufficient conditions are  known in the finite case. Several axiom systems have been shown to be sufficient in the general case. All of them include, or  imply, a very strong unnecessary assumption, (restricted) solvability. Tversky (1967) has given, for a more general problem, a  necessary and sufficient condition of an abstract nature, since it is actually a property of an extension of the weak order to  an algebraic structure in which the given set is embedded.  We shall show that a necessary condition, H, is sufficient in the countable case; then, we shall extend this result to a  noncountable Cartesian product, when another necessary condition, perfect separability, is added. Though condition H is a  property of the given weakly ordered set, it is abstract in nature, and its interpretation as both an independence  andAchimedean condition is bestseen on the extensions described above.  However, condition H can be showed to hold, or not to hold, in many applications. An example is given from an axiom system not  implying any form of solvability for which H is proved to hold, hence an additive utility is proved to exist.  1 I thank the referees for their helpful comments and suggestions.
