The condition (3.1), that < is a negatively transitive relation on Ml X M,, is a wellknown essential condition to prove the  existence of a <-representing utility function (see Fishburn, 1970, Theorem 3.1, p. 27). Therefore the problems which may  arise, e.g. by its implication of transitive indifference, need not be discussed again. At first glance the axioms (3.2) and  (3.3) seem to be very natural, because if f (a, xl , x2) is interpreted as a random experiment in which xl is obtained with  probability a and x2 with probability 1 - a, then (3.2) says, for every y e M2 the random experiment (f (a, xl , x2), y) is at  least as good as the least preferred and at most as good as the most preferred of the outcomes (xl , y), (_v2, y).
