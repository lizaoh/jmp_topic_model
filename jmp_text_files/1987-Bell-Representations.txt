This  reduces the assessment task to one of finding two one-dimensional scales. The natural generalization of this idea to n  attributes has been to assume unconditional ordinal additivity, which requires the assessment of n one-dimensional scales, or  a hierarchy of additive subsets, requiring as many as 2n - 2 scales.  It is clear that if, for each subset, we permit a multilinear representation rather than simply an additive representation  then the resulting set of decompositions is broader and thus more flexible, yet without requiring the assessment of any more  scales.
