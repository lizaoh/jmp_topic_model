To summarize, we have suggested that the parameterization of (2) developed by Greeno be replaced by a more tractable set of parameters. For the general case, x and y should be replaced by yi and p. These latter parameters are better than the former in that they are probabilities and their possible values are known. For restricted versions of (1), yl and p should be replaced by the appropriate subset of {8, z, h}. These parameters are also probabilities (though not of observable events); their ranges are known, and any identifiable parameters of (1) are easily expressed as functions of the suggested parameters of (2).
