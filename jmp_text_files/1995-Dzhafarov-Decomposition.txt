ORDINAL CHOICE FUNCTIONS 

A straightforward line of further mathematical development would consist in considering choice functions with progressively weaker constraints imposed on their positive domains. Moving along this line, one would construct progressively more general criteria of m-separability, delimit the corresponding classes of admissible transformations for m-generating functions, and investigate conditions under which these transformations yield p.m.f’s. The mathematical complexity (or at least cumbrousness) of this task seems to be formidable. It is worthwhile, therefore, to discuss an approach according to which such an investigation, interesting as it might be from a purely mathematical point of view; may be unnecessary for probabilistic representations of recurrent choices. 

As stated in the introduction, the notion of recurrent choices involves a chronological (or quasi-chronological)
