JOURNAL OF MATHEMATICAL PSYCHOLOGY 36, 450-460 (1992)  Statistical Theory for the Ratio Model of Paired Comparisons  JACQUES CARRIERS  The University of Iowa  AND  MARK FINSTER  The University of Wisconsin-Madison  This paper investigates the ratio model for paired comparisons. This model arises in Saaty's (1977, Journal of Mathematical  Psychology, 5, 234-281) scaling method for priorities in hierarchical structures and it also arises in the constant sum paired  comparison models presented by Hauser and Shugan (1980, Operations Research, 28, 278-320). This paper characterizes some  experimental designs that yield an estimable ratio scale and it develops a uniformly minimum variance, unbiased estimator of  the scale parameters. An estimator is developed when only a subject of all p(p-1)/2 comparisons are made and when the  comparisons are correlated. Moreover an intercept version of the standard model is investigated and tests of consistency and  reliability are presented. Dc 1992 Academic Press, Inc.  1. CONCLUSIONS  This paper focuses on the ratio model for paired comparisons that is used by Saaty (1977) and by Hauser and Shugan (1980). We  show how to estimate the parameters in the ratio model when only a subset of all comparisons are made. Specifically it is  shown that the scale parameters can be estimated up to a similarity transformation with an UMVU estimator. This estimator is  developed when the responses are correlated. Moreover we introduce an intercept version of the standard non-intercept model  and we present estimators for both the intercept and non-intercept versions of the ratio model. Finally, tests of consistency  and reliability are presented.  7. APPENDIX: PROOFS  Proof of Lemma 1. Let c be an n x 1 circuit vector that is not orthogonal to 1.
