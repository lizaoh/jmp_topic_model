INTRODUCTION AND STATEMENT OF PROBLEMS  The principal objectives of this paper are, first, to study the algebraic error-sensitive qualitative probability relations on  Boolean algebras, and, secondly, to prove a representation theorem for finitely additive semiordered qualitative probability  structures.  It will be assumed that the reader is familiar with the basic facts of qualitative (subjective) probability theory.  The knowledge in this field has expanded prodigiously since 1937 when B. de Finetti presented the basic conditions for  qualitative probability. An indication of this expansion can be gained from the survey and extensive bibliography in Luce &  Suppes (1965) or Domotor (1969). CONCLUDING REMARKS AND OPEN PROBLEMS  A question arises of what group or set T of transformations the probability measure in Theorem 3 is unique up to; or in other  words, `how many' different probability measures can we have, one a binary relation } in a FASQP structure is given. We might  expect some periodic functions with period e to be the elements of the unknown set T. The complete answer does not seem to be  simple, and we therefore leave it as an open problem.  We pointed out that the intransitivity of the relation reflects the inability of a measurement method (or apparatus) to  distinguish or recognize two different magnitudes of the measured quantity, when their difference is below the sensitivity of  the method. More refined measurement methods are needed to make these magnitudes distinguishable.
