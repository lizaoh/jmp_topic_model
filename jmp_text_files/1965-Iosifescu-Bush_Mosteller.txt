THEODORESCU  Academy of the Roumanian People's Republic, Center of Mathematical Statistics, Bucharest, Roumania  Asymptotic results are given for Bush-Mosteller models and their generalizations. Detailed proofs are given elsewhere.  It is known that a systematic treatment of models occurring in mathematical learning theory was given by Bush and Mosteller  (1955). Though these authors were not aware of the theory of processes with complete connections initiated by Onicescu and  Mihoc (1935) they used in their monograph such processes, and thereby developed the most important field of application of  this theory.' From the Markovian point of view, an important contribution to learning theory was made by Suppes and Atkinson  (1960).  Taking into account this tight relationship between the theory of processes with complete connections and the Bush-Mosteller  stochastic models for learning, we tried to investigate some theoretical aspects of this theory from the point of view of  these models.  Thus, in Iosifescu andTheodorescu (1962b) and Theodorescu(1960), we have shown that the Bush-Mosteller stochastic models for  learning may be obtained by means of schemes involving processes with complete connections; further, we gave some models for  learning generalizing the experimenter, subject- and experimenter-subjectcontrolled events and the functional relations  verified by moments of different orders corresponding to these models as well as the effective expression of the limit of  these moments as the number of experiments tends to infinity (see also Mihoc (1963) for Monte Carlo techniques concerning the  calculation of these limits). In the same direction we mention the note by Iosifescu (1963a) devoted to the study of learning  models with an arbitrary set of states as well as the paper by Lamperti and Suppes (1959).  At the same time we developed (see Iosifescu (1963b, 1964), and Iosifescu and Theodorescu (1962a, 1964b)) the theory of simple  and multiple processes with complete  ' For details on this theory, see Ciucu and Theodorescu (1960), which contains almost all the material published in this field  up to 1960.  196  STOCHASTIC MODELS FOR LEARNING 197  connections, especially for an arbitrary set of states (see also Ionescu Tulcea (1959)). Recently, we succeeded in extending  certain results to the nonstationary case (see losifescu and Theodorescu (1964a)), which seems to be very useful in further  investigations concerning stochastic models for learning.  In a forthcoming paper we intend to study some statistical aspects of the BushMosteller models from the point of view of  estimation theory.  The aim of this paper is to give a short survey of the last results obtained by the authors in the field of mathematical  learning theory for the case of a finite set of states; the extension to an arbitrary set of states does not raise principal  difficulties, as indicated inIosifescu(1963b), due to a new interpretation of the models.
