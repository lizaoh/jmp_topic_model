1. INTRODUCTION 

Systems for weak measurement attach numbers to objects in such a way that if the objects satisfy a particular empirical relation then their attached numbers satisfy a particular numerical relation. This correspondence may or may not be perfect because, in general, two objects may have the same number attached without being indifferent as regards the empirical relation. In the case that the correspondence is perfect we shall speak of strong measurement. 

Weak extensive measurement is useful, for instance, in collective decision making for the study of the representability of preference orderings defined in R[n] which satisfy some type of normative property, like zero independence. (See Moulin, 1988, p. 36). Such is the case of the lexicographic ordering, which can be interpreted as the existence of a dictator. It should be noted that although no strong measurement is possible for this ordering, a weak extensive measurement does exist. In utility theory contexts in which an ordered group appears have been studied in Chipman (1960, pp. 207 and ff). Also, in Fishburn (1974) contexts that lean on a lexicographic ordering have been analyzed. It is well known that some lexicographic orderings

Matching the context of orderings on semigroups with the search of pseudo-utility representations on ordered sets, we arrive to weak extensive measurement, whose translation could be pseudoutility representations on a totally preordered semigroup. Few systems of axioms for weak extensive measurement have been proposed in the literature. Among them, the system introduced in Holman (1969) (see also Colonius, 1978) is perhaps the most widely used. That system, nevertheless, is not characteristic of nontrivial weak extensive measure. Indeed, a subset of its axioms are sufficient, and one of them is necessary, but not all of them are necessary as well as sufficient. The search for a new system of axioms that characterize nontrivial weak extensive measurement has been the main objective in this paper. For the case of totally preordered indifference-semigroups, more general than Holman's context, we shall introduce one such system. In comparison with Holman's system, the key is that conditions of translation-invariance have been dropped.
