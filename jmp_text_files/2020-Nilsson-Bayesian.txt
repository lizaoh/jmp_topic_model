Nilsson, Rieskamp, and Wagenmakers (2011)  provided a hierarchical Bayesian parameter estimation procedure for cumulative prospect theory (CPT;  Tversky & Kahneman, 1992 ) and compared it against a standard maximum likelihood procedure. A parameter recovery analysis (Study 1) and an analysis of behavioral data (Study 2) demonstrated that though the two procedures generated similar parameter values at the aggregate level, the hierarchical Bayesian procedure was more robust against random error at the individual level. In this erratum, we identify (and analyze the effect of) a misspecification of the CPT model used for the hierarchical Bayesian procedure in Study 2 of  Nilsson et al. (2011) . The results of a reanalysis of the data using a corrected model are presented at the end.

Misspecification of CPT in  
                   Nilsson et al. (2011)

While the full version of CPT (Eqs.  (1) – (4) ) was used for the maximum likelihood procedure, a constrained version where all probabilities were weighted by Eq.  (3)  was used for the hierarchical Bayesian procedure. As mentioned, this leads to a situation where probabilities associated with relatively poor outcomes receive less weight than intended by the underlying theory in prospects involving either two positive or two negative outcomes. While the misspecification was unproblematic in Study 1, which only included mixed prospects, it might have biased the estimated parameter values in Study 2 that involved (1) choices between sure gain prospects, (2) choices between sure loss prospects, and (3) choices between mixed prospects. The effect of the misspecification on the observed parameters values is evaluated in the next section. Updated code for the hierarchical Bayesian procedure can be found at  https://osf.io/eypgb/ .
