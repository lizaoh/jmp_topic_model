The second motivating line originated in the psychophysical scaling developed by Gustav Fechner in the mid 1800s on the basis  of Weber's law. An implication of this approach is that s is perceived to be brighter than t if and only if O(s) > ~(t) + 1,  i.e., that luminosities can be scaled so that one is seen to be brighter than another if they differ by at least one just  noticeable difference. A nice account appears in Narens (1985, pp. 88-94). The idea of thresholds of discrimination in  judgments of preference was subsequently effected by Armstrong (1939) and Luce (1956). Luce's notion of semiorder, anticipated  to an extent by Armstrong as well as Norbert Wiener (see Wiener, 1914, 1915, or Fishburn and Monjardet, 1989), yields  representations similar to Weber's law. The semiorder notion for } on P was brought into linear utility by Luce (1956), and Vincke (1980) and Nakamura (1988) have  axiomatized semiordered generalizations of linear utility. Nakamura (1988, 1990b) also gives axioms for other cases of the  generalized linear model  p}q-u(p)>u(q)+6(q), a>0.  We say more about this at the end of the next section.  The integration of thresholds into nonlinear utility theory was initiated by Nakamura (1990a) with axioms for the model  p~- q-O(p, q) > w(p, q)  in which 0 is an SSB functional, w is a symmetric [w(q, p) = w(p, q)] bilinear functional, w(p, q) > - 10 (p, q) I when 0 :A  0, and w(p, q) > 0 when 0 = 0. We regard this as an important development since it incorporates the notion of imprecise  discriminability in judgments of preference into a tractable mathematical model that allows preferences to be intransitive  (May, 1954; Tversky, 1969; Fishburn, 1988) and does not presume independence axioms that are often observed to be  violated in choice experiments (Allais, 1953, 1979; Morrison, 1967; Kahneman and Tversky, 1979; MacCrimmon and Larsson, 1979).  The modified SSB model with constant threshold considered here is the obvious w- 1 specialization of Nakamura's (0, w) model.  As such, it is more vulnerable to refutation than the (0, w) model. Indeed, the final paragraph in the next section gives one  context in which it is quite unlikely to hold. Nevertheless, we believe it is of interest from both the psychological and  mathematical perspectives to examine the axiomatic foundations of the constant threshold SSB model.  Our axioms and main theorem appear in the next section. A few of the axioms duplicate ones in Nakamura (1990a), but new axioms  are needed for the constant threshold feature and our avoidance of Nakamura's assumption that } is unbounded. Our theorem  covers four cases for >- on P that depend on special structure. The most inclusive is the unrestricted representation that we  introduced at the outset.  The proof of the main theorem and comments on the independence of our axioms appear in Sections 3-5. A brief summary concludes  the paper.  Two remarks are in order before we proceed. First, the model axiomatized here is not a generalization of the SSB model because  of its positive threshold feature. In other words, the SSB model is not a special case of the new model. SUMMARY  Our purpose in this paper has been to identify axioms for a preference relation } on a convex set P of probability measures  that are necessary and sufficient for the constant threshold model  p}q-0(p, q) > 1,  where 0 is a skew symmetric bilinear functional on P x P. Eight axioms were specified for the model. The final two are  admittedly awkward but are independent of the first six and cannot be deleted. However, if either } is unbounded or one of  Pmax = { p: p } r for all r E P } and Pmi= { p: r ~ p for all r E P } is empty, then the final two axioms are implied by the  others. Moreover, when >- is unbounded, the sixth axiom can be replaced by a simpler assumption.  We also considered the special case of the model that does not require 10(p, q)j <, 1 when both p and q are indifferent to  everything in P. In this case the first seven axioms are necessary and sufficient.  So long as >- is nonempty, 0 in each version of the model is unique.
