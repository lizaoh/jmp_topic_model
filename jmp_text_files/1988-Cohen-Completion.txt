", 1988 Academic Press, Inc.  INTRODUCTION  In this note, necessary and sufficient conditions are presented to Dedekind complete a Closed Positive Concatenation  structure. (The definitions of Cohen and Narens (1979) and Narens and Luce (1976) are assumed throughout; see below.)  Sufficient conditions have already been presented by Narens and Luce (1976), but no necessary and sufficient conditions have  so far been presented. In addition, it is shown that arbitrary Positive Concatenation Structures defined on a dense subset of  the positive reals cannot necessarily be Dedekind completed by exhibiting a new graphical method to verify the axioms.  The property of Dedekind completability of a Positive Concatenation Structure is crucial from the standpoint of measurement.  Measurement is usually assumed to take place on a continuum of points without any arbitrary gaps. It is unreasonable to  believe that psychological continua such as loudness, pain, and brightness or physical continua such as velocity can have  intrinsic gaps at which the concatenation of the structure cannot be defined. The axioms of a Positive Concatenation Structure  only lead to a representation of the structure in an order-dense subset of the positive reals. There is no loss of generality in assuming that a Positive Concatenation Structure is defined on an order-dense subset of the  positive reals. This is because any Positive Concatenation Structure may be so embedded (Narens and Luce, 1976). Moreover, any  two distinct embeddings of a Positive Concatenation Structure onto order-dense subsets of the positive reals define a monotone  map from one orderdense subset of the positive reals to another. Any such map can be extended to a monotone continuous map  from the positive reals to itself which will define an isomorphism between the Dedekind Completions of the two embeddings  should any such completion exist. Therefore up to isomorphism, the embedding of the Positive Concatenation Structure is  essentially unique, and any Dedekind Completion of one embedding is naturally isomorphic to a Dedekind Completion of another.
