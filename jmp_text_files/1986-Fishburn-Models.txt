They reappeared in the  axiomatization of Koop man (1940a, 1940b) and were used subsequently by Smith (1961, 1965), Good (1962 ), Fishburn (1964 ),  Dempster (1967, 1968 ), and Suppes (1974 ). CONCLUDING REMARKS  With a few exceptions, necessary and sufficient conditions on the comparative probability relation have been specified for the  interval models summarized at the end of Section 2, the semiorder models of Section 3, and the associated convex models of  Section 4. The exceptions are the regular semiorder model and the convex interval model, which invite further research. The  other main technical question  MODELS FOR COMPARATIVE PROBABILITY 241  left open is whether the virtually indigestible Axiom 4* can be simplified for the regular interval model.  It has been argued that the models presented in this paper are more appropriate for inexact subjective probability than  earlier models. This of course begs the question of the extent to which such models satisfy the comparative judgments of real  people and calls for empirical studies. I would urge others to pursue this.  Finally, the usefulness of the new models for inference and decision deserves consideration.
