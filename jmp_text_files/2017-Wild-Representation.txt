The primary purpose of this article is the application of compression techniques (previously explored by the author in other contexts) to accommodate knowledge structures with millions of states. That not only reduces storage space but also facilitates statistical analysis. In the framework of the more specific Learning Spaces these compression techniques naturally lead to “query learning” which constitutes the second theme of our article. Although the author’s expertise is skewed towards the first theme, the research directions proposed for the second are deemed to be fruitful.
