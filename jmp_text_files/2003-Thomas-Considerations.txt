It was suggested in  Thomas (1999)  that to estimate  d

                      ′ from two-choice response probabilities, one needs to assume a distance-classifier decision rule. That is, the decision process used by an observer places percepts into stimulus categories based on the (Euclidean) distance between the percept and the stimulus distribution mean. The observation seemed intuitively correct but was not shown rigorously.
