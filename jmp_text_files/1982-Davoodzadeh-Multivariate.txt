INTRODUCTION     paper we present three models for multivariate paired comparison  experiments with ties. In Section 2 two models having Rao-Kupper (1967) univariate  marginal tie probabilities are developed directly using the Farlie-Gumbel  Morgenstern (see Johnson & Kotz, 1975) approach with logistic marginal  distributions, while a third, having Davidson's (1970) univariate marginal tie  probabilities, is developed using the association between the Rao-Kupper and the  Davidson univariate cell probabilities. In choosing among the three models, the  experimenter is faced with the question of whether to choose a model which is a  direct extension of the Davidson-Bradley (1969) model for multivariate paired  comparison experiment in terms of association structure, but having Rao-Kupper  marginal probabilities, or to choose a model having general Farlie-Gumbel  Morgenstern association structure with Davidson marginal probabilities. The decision  must take into account the simplicity (or complexity) of the model in terms of its  structural form and its set of sufficient statistics, as well as which properties one  wishes the measures of association to possess. Since data analysis based on any of  the three models involves computer iteration programs for estimation and testing,  there is no overriding reason to choose one model over the others on the basis of  simplicity or sufficient statistics. However, the association structure in the tie model  resulting from the extension of Davidson's multivariate model without ties may be  preferred over the association structure in the other two models in measuring    0022-2496/82/030269-13SO2.00/0    ht (c 1982 by Academic Press, Inc.
