INTRODUCTION  In the sociology of small groups one frequently considers binary relations on the members of such groups arising from such  concpts as affection, esteem, influence, and contact. White, Boorman, and Breiger (1976) hypothesized that the important  feature of such binary relations or sets of relations is the existence of blocks of individuals such that no individual in one  block has the relation to any individual in some other block (in practice on looks for pairs of blocks such that the density  of such interblock relationships is below a certain threshold, not necessarily equal to zero).  Once the group has been divided into blocks, fairly small matrices, indexed on the set of blocks, can be obtained. The (i, j)  block of such a matrix is equal to 1 if and only if the density of ties of the-prescribed kind from members of the ith block  to members of the jth block exceeds the user-specified threshold. Otherwise the entry is set equal to zero. These matrices  describe the structure of the group for the given ties. See Arabie, Boorman, and Levitt (1978) for more recent work.  To study the way that two different ties on the same group are related, Boorman and White (1976) suggested that the semigroup  of Boolean matrices generated by the matrices corresponding to the two ties, be studied. They remarked that the sizes of such  semigroups tended to vary greatly with the number of ones in the matrix. Their footnote 22 (p. 1417) in which they discuss  this question, can be regarded as the starting point of the present paper.  In general, the problem of the distribution of sizes of 2-generator subsemigroups of the semigroup Bof n X n Boolean  matrices appears quite difficult.
