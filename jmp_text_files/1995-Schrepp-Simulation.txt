Adaptive procedures for the construction of knowledge spaces by querying experts recently have been developed (see for example Dowling, 4993 or Koppen, 1993). in these procedures an expert judges assertions on the set of underlying questions. The procedures minimize the number of such assertions necessary for the construction of a knowledge space by drawing inferences from the judgements already obtained, The question is what happens if an expert judges some of these assertions incorrectly? Incorrect inferences are drawn from the incorrect judgernents, which then have an influence on the resulting knowledge space. Clearly, querying experts is a very effective method for constructing knowledge spaces. However, as our simulation study has shown, the influence of errors on the resulting — spaces has to be investigated before these spaces can be used as a basis for the effective assessment of knowledge. 

Another approach is to refine the querying procedure in order to reduce the error probabilities or to allow the detection of inconsistencies by the expert during the querying Process. 

Obviously erroneous acceptances are more critical for the procedure than erroneous rejections. Therefore one idea is to reduce the probability « of an erroneous acceptance at the . eye cost of an increase of the probability £ of an erroneous rejection. 

Such an approach is described in Kambouri et al. (1994), where experts had to judge the presented assertions on an unequally scaled 3-point scale with numbers 1, 4, and 5.
