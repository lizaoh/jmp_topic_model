INTRODUCTION  Green and Luce (1974; Luce & Green, 1974a, b) discuss the response ratio hypothesis as a means of explaining sequential  dependencies in magnitude estimation responses. In their version of the hypothesis, these dependencies are effectively  constant over trials, and are really stimulus effects. We present a slight generalization of the hypothesis that also  incorporates true response dependencies.  Paraphrasing Luce and Green (1974b), let Sbe a random variable representing the signal presented on trial n, and let Rbe  the number (a random variable) emitted by the subject on trial n under typical magnitude estimation instructions. Upon  presentation of signal s, two independent (random variable) representations X(s), X*(s) arise, the former being the  immediately available representation, the latter being available on the next trial if reference to signal s is required.
