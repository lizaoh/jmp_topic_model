All real relational structures of scale type (0, n) with n < x have automorphism groups abstractly isomorphic to; moreover,  each contains subchain of order type theta, invariant under the action of the automorphism group, s.t. the action of the  automorphisms on this subchain is conjugate to that of a subgroup of the affines. 1 1987 Academic Press. Inc.  We present a characterization of a wide class of subgroups of the automorphism group of the ordered real line. This  characterization has applications in the foundations of measurement: see, for example Narens (1981a, 1981b), Luce and Narens  (1985). Most of these papers address the problems slightly differently, in terms of real relational structures and their  automorphism groups, but any automorphism group of a real relational structure is just a group of order-preserving  homeomorphisms of the real line, and the classification here does not depend at all on the specific relations that induced the  automorphisms. This transformation of the problem, while mathematically convenient, leads to some perverse definitions--for  example, scale type should not properly be a property of a group of homeomorphisms, but of the relational structure that  induces such a group-but they should not cause any serious conceptual difficulties.  The work here is largely self-contained-hence the fairly elementary topological definitions-but the basic theorems about  ordered groups and homeomorphisms are merely quoted. On occasion, results established for the ordered real line are applied to  structures which are order-isomorphic to it (i.e., structures of order type theta): in such places, the words  "order-preserving homeomorphism" and "orderautomorphism" are used more or less interchangeably.  It should be noted that this paper is just the mathematical solution of a problem posed to the author in mathematical terms.
